# CRS365 AI Automation Blueprint Fulfillment Checklist

## Before accepting payment

- Confirm one workflow is named and bounded.
- Confirm the workflow owner will attend the kickoff.
- Confirm access to representative inputs, outputs, and current process information.
- Confirm no legal, cybersecurity, compliance, procurement, or custom-development opinion is being sold.
- State that implementation is separate.
- Record the agreed business objective and scope in the agreement.

## After payment

- Send receipt, engagement agreement, intake, and kickoff link.
- Create the client folder.
- Duplicate the Blueprint Word template.
- Set the five-business-day delivery date.
- Add the 45-minute findings review to the calendar.
- Record source, Fit Score, top recommendation, and booked date in Brevo or the operating tracker.

## Kickoff preparation

- Review the intake before the call.
- Mark missing materials.
- Prepare a current-state workflow draft.
- List assumptions requiring confirmation.
- Identify sensitive data and mandatory human approvals.
- Choose the baseline metric to confirm.

## 60-minute kickoff

1. Confirm the business objective.
2. Walk the current workflow from trigger to completed output.
3. Confirm roles, tools, inputs, decisions, exceptions, and handoffs.
4. Validate friction points and baseline.
5. Agree on what remains human-controlled.
6. Confirm the success measures.
7. Restate what is included and excluded.
8. Confirm delivery and review dates.

## Analysis and drafting

- Complete the current-state workflow.
- Rank bottlenecks by impact, frequency, risk, and effort.
- Score data, process, ownership, integration, and governance readiness.
- Design the future-state workflow.
- Identify the minimum useful automation.
- Recommend tool roles before recommending new purchases.
- Define exception handling and approval controls.
- Create the 30-day roadmap with owners and acceptance criteria.
- Check that recommendations fit stated budget and technical comfort.
- Remove unsupported performance or savings claims.
- Quality-check spelling, page layout, tables, links, and client details.

## Findings review

- Lead with the recommendation and why it matters.
- Walk the current and future workflows.
- Explain what should remain human-controlled.
- Review the first 30 days.
- Confirm the owner and success metric.
- Answer clarification questions.
- Discuss implementation only after the Blueprint is understood.

## Closeout

- Deliver editable DOCX and final PDF.
- Allow one clarification revision requested within seven days.
- Send the revised files within two business days.
- Ask for permission to follow up about implementation.
- Record the next-step decision.
- Request a testimonial only after the client has used the Blueprint and can speak from experience.

## Scope-change rule

Pause and re-scope when the work expands to another workflow, more than five core applications, data migration, software configuration, custom development, vendor procurement, security testing, legal analysis, or extended stakeholder facilitation.
