The tool is not the strategy

A good tool can make a strong workflow faster. It can also make a broken workflow fail faster.

Before you buy, connect, or automate anything, write down four parts of the work:

1.Trigger: What starts the process?
2.Inputs: What information must be accurate?
3.Decision: What requires judgment or approval?
4.Handoff: Who needs the output, and where should it go?

Once those answers are clear, your top recommendation becomes much easier to evaluate. You can see what it should own, what should remain human, and whether it fits the systems you already use.
