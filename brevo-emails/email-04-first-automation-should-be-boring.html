The best first automation is rarely the flashiest one. It is usually repetitive, visible, rules-based, and annoying enough that everyone notices when it improves.

Good first candidates often look like this:

+Route new inquiries to the right person and confirm follow-up.
+Turn meeting notes into assigned action items and a customer recap.
+Move approved form data into the system your team already uses.
+Compile a weekly operations summary from trusted data sources.
Boring is useful. It gives you a clear baseline, a measurable result, and a contained place to learn before expanding.

A first project should earn trust. Once the team sees that the workflow saves time without creating new confusion, the next improvement becomes much easier to approve.
